Lucid

Mobile application that turns your photos into art and videos into animations in the styles of famous artists. The algorithm we have used are based on the most recent discoveries in the field of visual and image processing.

Some of the techniques we employ:

  • Convolutional Neural Networks where we plug convolutional layers to mix the style of the artist into the original image
  • Relatively shallow custom Resnet to memorize/cache a “style definition” and then be able to transform any image into an art picture in under a second on a GPU (above K10 series above)
  • For the videos we use some advanced algorithms for blending the separate frames thus avoiding the flicker effect of each frame drawn separately

Craze

Mobile application that helps you find the most interesting events in town.

We have used Multinomial Naive Bayes classificatory for the automatically scanned events to categorise them into one of the 20 categories — Music, Sports, Business achieving 92% accuracy with the event in English. That allowed us to learn the interests of the users and to start to recommend events that fit their preferences.

We were also able to determine the trending events in town and suggest the most relevant events to our users.

Some of the techniques and research we have employed are:

  • language classifier
  • xml/html tag stripper; punctuation stripper 
  • regular expressions to remove junk
  • lemmatizers/stemmers
  • unicode accents removal
  • stop words removal & usage of bigrams/trigrams

Craze

Some of the classifiers we have experimented with:

  • Stochastic Gradient Descent Classifier
  • Naive Bayes & Multinomial Native Bayes Classifiers
  • After delving deeper in the details of the research papers we found out that Multinomial Native Bayes is performing best for the task, which we also confirmed empirically later
  • We have also developed anomaly detection algorithms and recommendation system with the help of a custom function that models the problem and behavior at hand

Some of the debugging techniques and metrics we have used:

  • events distribution analysis
  • precision, recall, f1-score
  • support
  • accuracy
  • confusion matrix
  • most meaningful words in a category
  • samples for not-well-classified events

The above techniques and the smart ways of creating training datasets allowed us to develop classifiers in Bulgarian with 89% accuracy without having to collect events database in Bulgarian.
